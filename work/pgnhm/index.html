Butterfly Town Exhibit

Pacific Grove is famous for being an important part of the annual migration of the Monarch butterfly. The Museum of Natural History in Pacific Grove invited me to redesign the entry way highlighting this inspirational migration. I layered graphic design, photography, artifacts, video and sculpture into a permanent multistory instillation at the museum.

Planning the exhibit

I begin by drawing the entry way in a cad program, to scale. This allowed me to quickly mockup my vision to the staff and scale the design and necessary cabinetry.

Digital layouts

I then was able to mockup the layout to scale over photography of the existing museum space. I also created custom graphics and wrote copy explaining the interesting science and history of the Monarch.

Papercraft sculpture

The centerpiece of the exhibit was a thousand butterfly, papercraft sculpture giving a life-size representation of the population decline over the past 20 years. This papercraft butterfly was designed by me. Then cut on a laser cutter and folded and mounted for display in the museum.
