Scientific Illustration

Communication is critical for scientists, conservation organizations and museums and my background in Illustration facilitates that communication.

Summarizing research

Often illustrations are important for summarizing larger bodies of research. This can help researchers communicate during conferences, for their thesis, or for grants. The design process is useful here, helping to discover and find the best way to communicate visually and quickly the important insights from a body of research.

This approach can also be helpful to explain to funders plans for future conservation work. Sometimes this is work that is already underway and we want to illustrate the potential of the work at scale.

Species

Illustration is a powerful tool to connect people to species important for conservation work. Visuals of the species we research develop understanding, concern, and empathy. They can also help us understand complex relationships among organisms.

I can provide illustrations as a toolset for researchers and conservationists who can incorporate them in presentations, applications, papers, and other productions.

Illustrations can help guide projects and installations, unifying stakeholders on a shared vision.

Illustrations can also help unify conservation organizations through identity work.
